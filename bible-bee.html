Rooted in the Word • Preschool & Elementary

What Is Rooted?

Rooted is Westminster's Bible engagement and memorization program for Preschool through 5th grade. Students are not just informed by Scripture; they are formed by it. Through consistent memorization, study, and community, the Word of God takes root in young hearts for a lifetime.

Our Goal

To help every student hide God's Word in their heart, building a foundation of Scripture knowledge that shapes how they think, love, and live, from the earliest years all the way through graduation and beyond.

"I have hidden your word in my heart that I might not sin against you."
Psalm 119:11
Grade-Level Programs
Preschool • PK3 & PK4
"Hear the Word"

Introduce students to the joy, rhythm, and truth of Scripture through repetition, song, movement, and simple Bible truths.

  • Recognize that the Bible is God's Word
  • Hear and repeat short Scripture phrases
  • Connect Bible truths to kindness, thankfulness, and prayer
  • Participate joyfully in chapel and call-and-response moments
Recognition: Rooted Blessing Certificate, sticker, or chapel shout-out
Kindergarten & 1st Grade
"Remember the Word"

Build confidence with short Scripture memory, Bible story recall, and understanding that God's Word teaches us who God is and how we live.

  • Memorize short verses or selected verse portions
  • Retell key Bible stories in simple language
  • Identify basic Bible truths: God made me, God loves me, Jesus saves
  • Practice speaking Scripture aloud with confidence
K-1 Showcase: classes recite verses and share a Bible truth in chapel (no competition)
2nd Grade
"Grow in the Word"

Introduce students to the formal Bible Bee track through Scripture memory, Bible facts, and simple comprehension.

  • Memorize assigned Bible verses and identify their reference
  • Answer simple Bible knowledge questions
  • Demonstrate understanding of what a verse means
  • Participate in a friendly grade-level Bible Bee round
Entry competition level in the annual Bible Bee
3rd Grade
"Understand the Word"

Strengthen biblical literacy by helping students memorize Scripture, understand context, and explain how God's Word applies to life.

  • Recite assigned verses with reference and increasing accuracy
  • Identify key people, places, and events in Scripture
  • Explain the meaning of selected verses in their own words
  • Connect Scripture to choices, character, and faith
Developing competition level in the annual Bible Bee
4th Grade
"Stand on the Word"

Prepare students to handle Scripture with greater confidence by memorizing verses, understanding biblical themes, and articulating truth clearly.

  • Recite longer or more complex Scripture passages
  • Identify themes: creation, sin, redemption, obedience, grace, faith
  • Explain how Scripture shapes Christian living
  • Model confidence and leadership in Bible engagement
Upper competition level in the annual Bible Bee
5th Grade
"Lead with the Word"

Give 5th grade students leadership opportunities connected to the Bible Bee without making them the primary competitors.

  • Serve as Bible Bee Ambassadors, Verse Buddies, readers, or hosts
  • Encourage younger students in Scripture memory
  • Model reverence, confidence, and joy in God's Word
  • Optional advanced Bible challenges and leadership devotionals
Leadership recognition rather than competition awards

Study Tips for Families
Repetition

Say It Together

Read the verse aloud together at breakfast, in the car, and at bedtime. Hearing it spoken in a trusted voice anchors it faster than reading alone.

Song

Sing It

Many of our weekly verses have musical versions. Singing a verse dramatically increases retention and makes memorization feel like play rather than work.

Meaning

Talk About What It Means

Ask your child what they think the verse means. Connect it to something real in their day. When Scripture connects to life, it moves from memory into formation.

Campus-Wide Rooted Goals

Spiritual Formation

  • Grow in love for God's Word
  • Understand that Scripture is true, trustworthy, and useful
  • Learn that Bible memory helps us follow Christ
  • Connect Scripture to daily life, choices, relationships, and worship

Biblical Literacy

  • Learn key Bible verses
  • Recognize major Bible stories, people, and themes
  • Understand the basic structure of the Bible
  • Build confidence using Bible language and references

Communication

  • Practice speaking clearly and confidently
  • Recite Scripture with reverence
  • Answer questions thoughtfully
  • Encourage others as they learn

Community

  • Celebrate Scripture together as a campus
  • Encourage classmates and younger students
  • See Bible engagement as part of who we are at Westminster
  • Connect chapel, classroom, and home through shared Scripture
