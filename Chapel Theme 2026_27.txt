Echoes of Eden
Every experience in life, both good and broken, finds its origin in the opening chapters of Scripture. In the Book of Genesis 1 through 3, we see the world as God designed it, full of beauty, order, purpose, and life. Every system, every natural law, and every expression of creativity flows from the words spoken by God at creation. Yet we also see where it fractured. Pain, suffering, and sin entered through a deceptive whisper and humanity’s decision to grasp for what was never theirs to take. 
Eden, then, is not just a place in the past. Its echoes are all around us. 
 
In our 2026-27 theme, Echoes of Eden, we will learn to see those echoes with greater clarity. Together, we will interpret life through the story God has been telling from the beginning. In our elementary chapels, students will follow Eden and Ed, two sheep siblings who experience the world much like we do, sometimes responding rightly and sometimes not, helping bring these deep truths to life in ways that are both engaging and formative. 
 
In middle school and high school chapels we will be making strategic stops through Scripture, from Genesis to Revelation, touching on important stories that weave the narrative of God’s redemptive work through the whole story of God.  
 
Together, we will trace the reverberations of the Fall and the echoes of God’s creative Word all the way to the cross of Jesus Christ, where sin was defeated and victory was secured. And as we do, we will hold fast to the promise declared in our verse for the year, Ecclesiastes 3:11 which says, “God has made everything beautiful in its time. He has also set eternity in the human heart; yet no one can fathom what God has done from beginning to end.” 
 
It's a great time to be a Warrior!
